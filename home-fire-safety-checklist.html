LPG hose is within 5-year replacement date Critical
Check the manufacturing date stamped on the hose. Rubber degrades and cracks. Replace every 5 years regardless of appearance.
No cloth, paper, or plastic near the hob or gas burner Critical
Dish towels, curtains, and packaging within 30 cm of a lit hob are the most common kitchen fire ignition source.
LPG cylinder valve is fully closed when not cooking High
Leaving the cylinder valve open — even with the burner off — allows gas to accumulate in the event of a regulator or hose failure.
Exhaust fan or window provides ventilation while cooking High
Adequate ventilation disperses gas accumulation and reduces grease build-up on surfaces near the hob.
Cooking oil is never left unattended on a hot hob High
Unattended cooking oil reaching smoke point (typically above 200°C) is the leading cause of kitchen fires in India. Turn off flame before leaving.
CO₂ extinguisher available for electrical appliance fires High
Fire blanket covers oil fires. CO₂ covers microwave, toaster, fridge motor. Never use ABC powder near food surfaces.

Electrical Safety
Second most common cause of home fires
0/8
MCB / RCD distribution board is labelled for each circuit Critical
In a fire or electrical fault you must be able to isolate the affected circuit within seconds. Unlabelled boards cause fatal delays.
No extension board is connected to another extension board (daisy-chaining) Critical
Daisy-chaining multiplies current draw beyond the extension board's rated capacity. This is the most common cause of extension board fires.
No exposed or damaged wire insulation anywhere in the home Critical
Inspect behind appliances, under carpets, and at all wall entry points. Damaged insulation causes shorts and arcing — the primary electrical fire cause.
RCD / ELCB tested within last 3 months (press test button) High
Press the test button on your ELCB / RCD. It should trip immediately. If it does not trip, it has failed and must be replaced — it provides no protection.
No appliances are left on overnight unnecessarily (TV, washing machine, EV charger exception noted) High
Most electrical fires occur between midnight and 6am when occupants are asleep. Use timer switches or smart plugs for overnight charging where needed.
Extension boards are IS 1293:2019 rated and not overloaded High
Check the maximum wattage rating on the extension board. Total load of connected appliances should not exceed 80% of rated capacity.
Main MCB location known by all adult household members High
Every adult must know exactly where the main MCB is and how to operate it before an emergency — not during one.
CO₂ extinguisher accessible near electrical panel / DB box High
Never use water or ABC powder on a live electrical fire. CO₂ only — accessible before approaching the panel.

Bedrooms
Where most fire fatalities occur — people asleep
0/8
Smoke detector fitted in each bedroom and tested this month Critical
NDMA recommends smoke detectors in all sleeping areas. Test the alarm button monthly. Replace battery annually or when indicator beeps.
Clear escape route from every bedroom identified and practised Critical
Every sleeping occupant must be able to exit the building in under 2 minutes in darkness. Walk the route. Identify secondary exit if primary is blocked by fire.
Phone is charged near bed so 101/112 is reachable at night High
In a fire at night, occupants have under 3 minutes before conditions become untenable. Having a charged phone within reach saves critical call time.
No mobile phones or laptops charged under pillow or mattress overnight High
Charging under bedding is the leading cause of bedroom fires among the 15–30 age group. Devices must be charged on hard surfaces with airflow.
Curtains are at least 30 cm from electrical sockets and switches High
Children know the family fire escape plan and assembly point High
Practice the escape plan twice a year. Children should know: never hide, get low under smoke, feel door before opening, go to the assembly point and do not re-enter.
No candles, diyas, or agarbatti left burning unattended or overnight High
Never fall asleep with a lit open flame in the room. Diya fires in bedrooms during Diwali are a well-documented seasonal risk in Indian homes.
Bedroom doors are closed at night (slows fire spread significantly) Standard
A closed bedroom door can hold back a fire for 10–15 minutes — giving critical evacuation time. Fire doubles in temperature every minute in an open room.

Fire safety in Indian homes: what the data shows

According to NCRB data, residential fires account for the largest share of accidental fire deaths in India. The three most common causes are: (1) unattended cooking — oil fires and LPG accidents, (2) electrical faults — overloaded circuits, damaged insulation, and faulty extension boards, and (3) negligent use of open flames — candles, diyas, and agarbatti near flammable materials.

The critical window in any residential fire is 3–5 minutes. A room reaches fatal temperatures in under 3 minutes once a fire establishes. Smoke inhalation, not burns, causes the majority of fire fatalities — which is why smoke detectors in sleeping areas and closed bedroom doors at night are the two highest-impact safety measures available for no cost.

The IS 2190:2024 standard (Code of Practice for Selection, Installation and Maintenance of Portable First Aid Fire Extinguishers) is the current mandatory maintenance standard for all extinguishers in India. The IS 15683:2018 standard (with Amendment No. 1, February 2025) is the current mandatory product standard. Both supersede earlier versions — IS 2190:2010 and IS 15683:2006 are withdrawn.
