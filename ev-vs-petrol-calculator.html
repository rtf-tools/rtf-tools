EV vs petrol scooter in India: what the numbers actually show

At ₹103/litre petrol and a 30 km daily commute, a standard petrol scooter (45 km/L) costs approximately ₹21,500/year in fuel alone. An equivalent electric scooter at 4 km per unit and ₹7/unit electricity costs approximately ₹3,900/year. The annual fuel saving is approximately ₹17,600 — before accounting for the maintenance differential (₹4,000/year petrol vs ₹1,500/year EV).

The total 5-year saving for a Bajaj Chetak 3001 (₹94,500) vs a mid-range petrol scooter (₹90,000) on this commute is approximately ₹97,000 — effectively getting the EV for free after 5 years of riding. Higher commute distances accelerate this dramatically: at 50 km/day the 5-year saving exceeds ₹1.5 lakh.

The real range of EV scooters in Indian city conditions is 65–75% of the IDC laboratory figure. A Bajaj Chetak 3001 with 127 km IDC range delivers 90–100 km in real city riding. An Ather Rizta S 3.7 kWh with 125 km IDC delivers 95–130 km in Smart Eco mode. This calculator uses conservative real-world efficiency figures — not manufacturer claims.

The PM e-Drive subsidy of ₹5,000 per vehicle is already built into Amazon.in listed prices and applies to all high-speed models including Chetak, Rizta, Magnus, and Nexus ST. It expires 31 July 2026.
